Adjectives

Adjectives of Quality

Adjectives are words that modify or provide more information about nouns. Adjectives of quality refer to words that show the nature of nouns in terms of beauty, ugliness, morals, and shapes.

For example:

  • She is a nice girl.
  • He is heavy.
  • John is a thin boy.
  • This job is difficult.

Usually, such an adjective has a variety of synonyms e.g. Huge—big, enormous, gigantic, vast etc.

Adjectives of Quantity

Adjectives of quantity are words that describe a noun by showing how many or how much of the noun is there or shows the number of the items.

Gradable Adjectives

Gradable adjectives describe qualities that can be measured or graded in some way. They are usually used with intensifiers like very, quite, fairly, terribly, rather, completely, absolutely, and really.

Non-Gradable Adjectives

Non-gradable adjectives describe either extreme qualities or absolute qualities and cannot be used with intensifiers like very.

Comparative Forms of Adjectives

Comparative forms of adjectives are used when we want to compare two people, things, or situations mainly by adding –er at the end of the adjective.

Superlative Forms of Adjectives

Superlative forms of adjectives are used to compare more than two items, people, or situations and show the most dominant or the best of the lot.

Irregular Adjectives

Irregular adjectives do not have a predictable pattern of forming comparatives and superlatives.
