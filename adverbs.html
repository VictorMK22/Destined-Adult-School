Adverbs

Adverbs of Time:

- Yesterday, she visited her grandmother.

- They will arrive here soon.

- He usually wakes up early in the morning.

Adverbs of Frequency:

- She always completes her tasks on time.

- He seldom goes to the gym.

- We occasionally meet for lunch.

Adverbs of Place:

- Come here and sit down.

- The cat is hiding underneath the table.

- They searched everywhere for the lost keys.

Adverbs of Manner:

- She sings beautifully.

- He walked slowly along the path.

- They danced gracefully at the ball.

Adverbs of Degree or Quantity:

- He was completely exhausted after the marathon.

- The movie was extremely boring.

- She is very excited about her upcoming vacation.

Adverbs of Reason:

- He left early because he had an appointment.

- She failed the exam due to insufficient preparation.

- He couldn't attend the meeting as he was feeling unwell.

Adverbs Used as Interrogatives:

- When will you finish your homework?

- Why did you leave the party early?

- How did they manage to solve the problem?

Adverbs Used as Adjectives or Adverbs:

- He spoke in a loud voice. (adjective)

- Donâ€™t talk so loud. (adverb)

- The weather turned cold suddenly. (adjective)

- She sings suddenly. (adverb)

Adverbs with and without '-ly':

- She works hard every day.

- Roma hardly works these days.

- He drives fast on the highway.

- The train arrived late at the station.

Adverbs Used as Nouns after Prepositions:

- He stays far from here. (far is an adverb)

- He comes from there. (there is an adverb)

- She lives nearby. (nearby functions as an adverb)
