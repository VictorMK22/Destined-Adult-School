Definition and Usage

A preposition is a word placed before a noun or a pronoun to show in what relationship the person or thing denoted by it stands in regard to something else. Prepositions are used to indicate location, time, manner, direction, and other relationships.

Here are some examples of how prepositions are used:

  • Place: about, at, against, among, below
  • Time: at, on, in, from, until
  • Agency: at, by, through
  • Manner: with
  • Cause/Reason: for, of, with, through
  • Possession: on, of, with
  • Standard: at (price), by (inches)
  • Motive: from what I know of him, I did it from gratitude

Prepositions can be simple, consisting of one word, or complex, consisting of more than one word. Complex prepositions express different meanings and are often made up of two or three words.

  1. No doubt he has achieved much but I cannot give him credit for all that he boasts of.

  4. It is difficult to agree with those critics who ascribe the work of Shakespeare to Bacon.
  5. The soldiers in the fort were provided with provisions to last them a year.
  6. We are accountable to God for our actions.
  7. Measure yourself with your equals and learn from frequent competition the place which nature has allotted to you.
  8. At the eleventh hour he retired from the contest leaving the field open to his opponents.
  9. Dogs have antipathy to cats.
  10. He has been very indulgent with his children.
