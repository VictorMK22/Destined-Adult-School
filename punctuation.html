Punctuation

Full Stop (Period):

- Mary is sick.

- He works at K.N.H.I.F and N.S.S.F.

Comma:

- She went to the market to buy bread, soap, and sugar.

- "I will see you tomorrow," she said.

- Daniel Arap Moi, the second president of the Kenyan republic, is visiting Nakuru National Library tomorrow.

Colon:

- You should bring the following items tomorrow: bananas, spoons, cups, knives, and plates.

- The Employment Act states: "Wages shall not be paid in a bar or at a place where intoxicating drinks are available for supply."

Semi-colon:

- This school is the oldest in Kenya; it was built in 1906.

- Don't come late; otherwise, you will miss the party.

Dash:

- I don’t know why she hasn’t arrived—I mean, she should have been here two hours ago!

- Jo—John has gone—gone ho—home.

- Boys can be sweet—when they want to be.

Apostrophe:

- Don't—do not.

- Maina's bag

- How are you doing—how’re you doin’?

Hyphen:

- Father-in-law

- Re-cover/recover

- Anti-Christ
