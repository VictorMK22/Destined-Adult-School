Nouns

A noun is a name of anything or simply, a naming word. Nouns are categorized into:

  • Proper nouns: Names of specific people, places, or things. Proper nouns always start with a capital letter. E.g., Kenya, Peter, Lake Victoria, Nairobi, etc.
  • Common noun: Names of people, places, and things that are not specific. E.g., desk, boy, river, plate, etc.
  • Concrete Nouns: Names of things, people, etc., that can be touched. E.g., book, desk, water, James, bottle, etc.
  • Abstract nouns: Names of things or concepts that cannot be touched or seen. E.g., wind, corruption, love, hate, God, etc.
  • Collective nouns: Names of groups of things. These kinds of names usually take a singular verb.

Count and non-count Nouns:

  • Count nouns: Names of things that can be counted. They occur in singular and plural forms. E.g., boy—boys, one boy—20 boys.
  • Non-count nouns: Refer to things that cannot be counted, like substances, qualities, ideas, liquids, and objects.

Irregular and Regular Nouns:

  • Regular nouns: Take predictable plural forms. E.g., cup—cups, toy—toys, shirt—shirts.
  • Irregular nouns: Do not take 's' or 'es' in plural form. E.g., man—men, woman—women, mouse—mice.

Collective Nouns:

  • These refer to a set or group of people, animals, or things. E.g., a crowd, a band, a choir.

Compound Nouns:

  • A compound noun is made up of more than one word and can be written as a single word, a hyphenated word, or separate words.
  • Compound nouns can take different forms based on whether the plural ending is added to the first or last word.
  • Examples include bookshop, ice cream, passer-by, father-in-law, etc.
