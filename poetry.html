Oral Poetry/Songs

An oral poem or a song is a composition in verse form expressing ideas and thoughts verbally. An oral poem can be sung, recited, or chanted.

Types of Songs/Oral Poems

  1. Birth/Cradle
    • Songs performed when a baby is born or sung to encourage a woman in labor.
  2. Child Naming
    • Songs sung during the naming of newborns.
  3. Lullabies
    • Songs sung to comfort a baby to sleep.

Characteristics of Songs

  1. They have repetitive segments.
  2. They have a direct address of the audience for immediacy of expression.
  3. They carry a direct translation of the original language for local flavor or authenticity.

Styles in Oral Poetry

  1. The pattern of the poem

    The pattern of the poem refers to the structure and layout.

  2. Diction

    The choice of words used in a song.

  3. Imagery

    Figures of speech like Metaphor, simile, and symbolism that create mental images.
